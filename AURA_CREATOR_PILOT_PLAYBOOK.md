# Aura Creator Pilot Playbook

> How to run the first 10 creator pilots manually before automating anything.  
> Updated: 2026-06-07

---

## What a pilot is

A pilot is a white-glove, manually-operated Superfan Club launch for one creator. The goal is not to prove the tech — it is to prove the loop:

1. Creator launches a club page.
2. Fans sign up and earn points through challenges.
3. Creator sees who their real fans are.
4. A local partner business sponsors a campaign.
5. Fans redeem a reward at the partner business.
6. Attribution is verified.

**Success = the creator says: "I know who my real fans are and I made money from it."**

---

## Ideal pilot creator profile

| Attribute | Target |
|---|---|
| Platform | Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube (any one is fine) |
| Follower count | 10K–200K |
| Engagement rate | ≥ 3% (at least 300 active commenters per 10K followers) |
| Location | Concentrated audience in one city (Paris, Lyon, Montréal, London, etc.) |
| Niche | Lifestyle, food, fitness, beauty, fashion, local culture |
| Motivation | Wants to monetize without brand deals; has tried Linktree/Beehiiv; curious about community tools |
| Availability | Can spend 2–3 hours in setup week; 30 min/week thereafter |

**Red flags:** Creators who only post viral content with no comment community. Creators who want to sell courses primarily. Macro creators who already have agency teams.

---

## Pre-pilot checklist

Before onboarding a creator pilot, confirm:

- [ ] Creator has ≥ 1,000 followers who regularly comment.
- [ ] Creator is willing to ask their audience to sign up for a free community.
- [ ] Creator can name 3 rewards they could realistically offer (e.g., DM access, early merch, free coffee from a local partner).
- [ ] Creator's city has ≥ 5 local businesses in their niche (coffee shops, gyms, boutiques, studios).
- [ ] Creator signs a simple pilot MoU: free access in exchange for feedback and a case study.

## Pilot week 1: Superfan Club setup

### Day 1 — Kick-off call (30 min)

- Explain what a Superfan Club is and what fans will experience.
- Agree on 3 challenges for the first month.
- Agree on 3 rewards for the first month.
- Collect creator's brand colors, logo and cover image.
- Set up club page at `aura.club/@handle` or creator's custom domain.

### Day 2 — Club page live

- Fan signup form active (email + optional WhatsApp).
- Points system configured: like = 5 pts, comment = 15 pts, share = 25 pts, save = 20 pts, challenge completion = 100–500 pts.
- Leaderboard live showing top 10 fans.
- 3 initial challenges published.
- 3 rewards listed (digital and/or physical).

### Day 3 — Creator announces club

- Creator posts to their main platform: "I just launched my Superfan Club. Join for free and earn points..."
- Creator shares signup link in bio, stories, pinned post and DMs.
- Aura tracks first 48h: signups, points earned, challenge activity.

### Days 4–7 — First fan activity monitoring

- Daily recap to creator: new fans, total points, top earners, challenge completions.
- Creator replies to fans who completed challenges (personal DM or comment).
- Troubleshoot any signup issues.

---

## Pilot month 1: Community activation

### Week 1–2 targets

- 20+ fan signups.
- 5+ challenge completions.
- Creator posts at least 3 times with Superfan Club mentions.

### Week 3–4 targets

- 50+ fan signups.
- 10+ challenge completions.
- First reward redemption (digital: DM access, exclusive content, etc.).
- Identify top 10 fans for possible leaderboard spotlight.

### Creator touchpoints

| Touchpoint | Cadence | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Activity recap | Weekly | Email or WhatsApp |
| Creator Q&A | Bi-weekly | 20-min call |
| Optimization suggestions | Monthly | Written report |
| Case study interview | End of month 2 | 45-min call |

## Pilot month 2: Partner campaign

### Finding a local partner

- Together with creator, identify 3–5 local businesses in the creator's niche and city.
- Warm pitch: "Creator X has 80 superfans in [city]. Would you sponsor a challenge that sends verified fans to your business?"
- Offer the first campaign at zero or low cost to prove attribution.

### Campaign setup

1. Create a `Campaign` in Aura with partner business, budget, reward and tracking method.
2. Generate a unique QR code or coupon code for the partner.
3. Publish a challenge: "Visit [Partner Business] and scan the QR code to earn 500 pts."
4. Creator announces the challenge to their community.

### Measurement

- QR scans or coupon redemptions at partner business.
- Points awarded to fans who completed the challenge.
- Report to partner: unique fans who visited, engagement rate, estimated value per visit.

---

## Pilot outcomes and case study

At the end of week 8, document:

1. Total fan signups.
2. Total points awarded.
3. Challenge completion rate.
4. Top 10 fans (anonymous if needed).
5. Partner campaign: visits, QR scans, coupon redemptions.
6. Creator quote about value received.
7. Revenue generated (if any).
8. What was manual vs. what should be automated.

This case study is the primary sales asset for the next 90 creators.

## Manual operations log

During pilot, track every manual action that the product should eventually automate:

| Manual action | Priority to automate |
|---|---|
| Setting up club page | P0 (week 1 of self-serve build) |
| Sending weekly recap email | P1 |
| Generating QR code for partner | P0 |
| Adding challenge completion manually | P0 |
| Sending points after fan action verified | P0 |
| Finding local partner businesses | P2 (B2B agent) |
| Drafting partner pitch | P2 (AI draft) |
| Generating partner report | P1 |
| Processing reward redemptions | P1 |

**Rule:** Never build automation before the manual version has worked at least 3 times.

## Pilot team responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Pilot lead | Creator relationship, weekly recap, Q&A calls |
| Product | Club page setup, challenge/reward configuration |
| Partner sales | Local business outreach, campaign setup |
| Data | Fan activity monitoring, QR/coupon tracking |

During the first 5 pilots, one person can cover all roles. After 5 pilots, split partner sales out.

---

## What not to pitch during the pilot

The pilot is about proving community activation. Do not mention:

- Blockchain or token infrastructure.
- Fan pass minting.
- B2B agent automation.
- AI-generated campaign recommendations.
- Pricing or paid tiers.

The creator is not paying. They are proving the loop. Pricing conversation happens after month 2 case study.
