# Aura — Business Model

## Core economic logic

Aura grows by helping creators onboard fans.

The first economic asset is not the sponsor marketplace.

The first economic asset is:

identified and activated fan communities.

Aura becomes valuable when it knows:

- who follows which creator;
- who engages repeatedly;
- who joins challenges;
- who refers others;
- who attends events;
- who redeems rewards;
- which fans overlap between creators;
- which local segments are commercially activable.

## Base model

Recommended model:

Free creator acquisition  
→ fan onboarding  
→ creator SaaS upgrade  
→ sponsored reward campaigns  
→ agency/label plans  
→ optional marketplace commission

Do not rely on commission first.

Small local campaigns often have low budgets. A 10% commission on a 200€ campaign is only 20€. Commission becomes interesting only after volume and repeatability.

## Free plan

Purpose:

Get creators to bring their fans into Aura.

Free features:

- public Superfan Club page;
- fan signup;
- basic points system;
- 3 active challenges;
- 3 active rewards;
- basic leaderboard;
- basic creator dashboard;
- Aura branding visible;
- limit: 100 or 250 fans.

Free plan promise:

Launch your Superfan Club in 7 days. Identify your real fans. Reward the people who help you grow. Start building a fan base you own.

## Sponsored reward campaigns

After creators have active fan bases, Aura can monetize businesses.

Business offer:

Sponsor a reward inside a creator’s Superfan Club. Drive measurable traffic from engaged fans. Track redemptions with QR/coupon codes.

Early pricing:

149€–499€ per campaign.

Possible split:

- Aura setup fee: 99€–199€;
- creator compensation: negotiated;
- business reward cost: paid by business;
- Aura commission later: 10–20%.

## Data monetization

Do not say:

“We sell fan data.”

Say:

“We help creators and partners activate consent-based fan segments.”

Monetizable products later:

- sponsor-ready audience segments;
- creator audience overlap;
- campaign attribution reports;
- fan engagement scores;
- local activation segments;
- cross-community affinity maps.

## Network effect

Aura becomes more valuable when:

- more creators bring fans;
- fans join multiple clubs;
- Aura sees overlap between communities;
- businesses can sponsor targeted rewards;
- creators can collaborate based on shared fans;
- agencies can manage portfolios.

Powerful future insight:

Creator A and Creator B share 18% of their top engaged fans.

This unlocks:

- collabs;
- joint events;
- co-sponsored campaigns;
- shared drops;
- local tours.

## First revenue path

1. Free pilot for 3 creators.
2. Each creator onboards 50–150 fans.
3. Aura produces a community activation report.
4. Offer upgrade to 49€/mois.
5. Add 1 local sponsor campaign at 149€–299€.
6. Prove redemptions.
7. Repeat.
